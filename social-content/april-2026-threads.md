Post date: [ ] _____________
================================================================

### Box 1:
Solana settles transactions in 400 milliseconds.

Faster than a credit card swipe. Cheaper than $0.001 per transaction.

Here's how it works 🧵

---

### Box 2:
Most blockchains are sequential. One block, then the next.

Bitcoin: 7 TPS
Ethereum: 15 TPS

For global payments, those numbers are unworkable.

---

### Box 3:
Solana asked a different question:

What if validators didn't need to agree on time?

What if time itself was cryptographically provable?

---

### Box 4:
Enter Proof of History.

A verifiable timestamp built into the blockchain. Validators process transactions in parallel because order is established before consensus begins.

---

### Box 5:
The result:

→ 65,000 TPS theoretical
→ 400ms finality
→ <$0.001 per transaction

Visa peaks at 24,000 TPS for comparison.

THREAD 4 - Wire Transfers vs Crypto - Blog: April 15, 2026
Post date: [ ] _____________
================================================================

### Box 1:
The real cost of a $1,000 international wire transfer is $60-90.

Most people only see the $35 stated fee.

Here's what banks don't tell you 🧵

---

### Box 2:
Wire transfers go through correspondent banks.

Each bank in the chain takes a cut.

Stated fee: $35
FX markup: $15-30
Receiving bank: $10-25
Total: 6-9% on a $1,000 transfer

---

### Box 3:
Then there's time cost.

Funds clear in 3-5 business days.

For a business with 30-day cash flow, you're locking up working capital for a week per transfer.

---

### Box 4:
The crypto comparison:

USDC on Solana = $0.001 fee, under 1 second
USDC on Ethereum = $1-3 fee, ~1 minute

99% cheaper than a wire. 100x faster.

---

### Box 5:
Wires still serve a purpose for institutional counterparties and large transfers.

For sending $1,000 to a freelancer? You're setting money on fire.

### Box 1:
Crypto gas fees confuse everyone.

Here's how to never pay too much again 🧵

---

### Box 2:
Gas is the unit of measurement for blockchain computational work.

Like postage. Base cost depends on transaction complexity. Then congestion drives the price up or down.

---

### Box 3:
Same USDC transfer, very different costs:

Solana: $0.001
Polygon: $0.01
Ethereum: $1-50 depending on network traffic

Choose your network wisely.

---

### Box 4:
Three ways to pay less:

→ Pick the cheapest network for your asset
→ Time transactions for off-peak hours
→ Batch multiple sends together

---

### Box 5:
Tools to check live fees:

→ Etherscan Gas Tracker (Ethereum)
→ Solana network status
→ Most wallets show estimated fees before you confirm
